Public Health Assessment
Evaluating the subway proposal through vulnerability, safety, and healthcare access.
Comparing existing conditions across 12 proposed station areas to identify where the subway would deliver the greatest public health benefit.
Two questions: who is most vulnerable, and where are health burdens greatest?
1
Social Vulnerability
Who is most at risk? CDC SVI identifies communities with fewest resources.
2
Traffic Safety
Where are crash burdens highest? Subway reduces car dependency.
3
Healthcare Access
Which stations are farthest from medical facilities? Subway improves reachability.
Methodology
Walkshed
10-Minute Walk Service Area
Network-based isochrones from OpenStreetMap pedestrian graph (OSMnx). All indicators measured within each station's walkshed — not radius buffers.
Indicator 1
Social Vulnerability Index · CDC SVI 2022
Pre-computed composite (0–1) at census tract level. Spatially joined to walksheds, averaged across intersecting tracts.
Indicator 2
Serious & Fatal Crashes · PennDOT 2024
Point-in-polygon count of severity ≤2 crashes within each walkshed. Philadelphia County only.
Indicator 3
Distance to Nearest HC Facility · OSM
Straight-line distance (km) from each station to the nearest hospital, clinic, or pharmacy.

Indicator 01 / 03
Map · CDC SVI 2022
Social Vulnerability Index
CDC SVI scores by census tract within each walkshed. Higher values indicate greater social vulnerability — communities with fewer resources to cope with external stressors.
Data: CDC/ATSDR SVI 2022 · Pennsylvania Census Tracts
10 of 12 stations serve communities with SVI > 0.75. Broad-Erie (0.975) and North 9th (0.953) rank highest. Broad-Erie (43.9%) and North 9th (37.3%) also have the highest car-free household rates.

Indicator 02 / 03
Map · PennDOT 2024
Crash Hotspot Analysis
Serious and fatal crashes (severity ≤2) within each station's 10-minute walkshed. Reduced car dependency from subway use could yield significant traffic safety co-benefits.
Data: PennDOT Crash Reportable Data 2024 · Philadelphia County
Bustleton records 18 crashes — nearly 3× the corridor mean of 6.2. Cottman (12) and Rising Sun (9) also well above average.

Indicator 03 / 03
Map · OSM Healthcare
Healthcare Access
Straight-line distance from each proposed station to the nearest hospital, clinic, or pharmacy. Greater distance means residents must travel further for medical care.
Data: OpenStreetMap Healthcare Facilities · Philadelphia
Southampton (2.42 km) and Red Lion (1.80 km) are farthest from any healthcare facility. The subway would dramatically improve reachability for these communities.

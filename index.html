Public Health Assessment
What does the subway mean for community health along the corridor?
This analysis evaluates public health equity impacts of the proposed Roosevelt Boulevard Subway across 12 station areas.

We ask: who benefits most, and where is the need greatest? Three indicators capture vulnerability, safety risk, and healthcare access deficit. A composite score integrates all three to rank stations by priority.
SVI + Crashes + HC Deficit → Priority Score
Walkshed
10-Minute Walk Service Area
Network-based isochrones from OpenStreetMap pedestrian graph. All indicators are measured within each station's walkshed — not radius buffers.
Indicator 1 · CDC SVI 2022
Social Vulnerability Index
Pre-computed CDC composite (0–1) covering socioeconomic status, household composition, minority status, and housing/transportation. Spatially joined to walksheds at census tract level.
Indicator 2 · PennDOT 2024
Serious & Fatal Crashes
Point-in-polygon count of severity ≤2 crashes within each walkshed. Subway reduces car dependency → fewer vehicles → fewer crashes.
Indicator 3 · OSM Healthcare
Healthcare Access Deficit
Count of hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies within each walkshed — inverted so fewer facilities = higher deficit.
Composite = SVI×40 + (crashes/18)×35 + HC_deficit×25
Indicator 01 / 03
Map · CDC SVI 2022
Social Vulnerability Index
CDC SVI scores by census tract within each walkshed. Higher values indicate greater social vulnerability — communities with fewer resources to cope with external stressors.
Data: CDC/ATSDR SVI 2022 · Pennsylvania Census Tracts
10 of 12 stations serve communities with SVI > 0.75. Broad-Erie (0.975) and North 9th (0.953) rank highest. Broad-Erie (43.9%) and North 9th (37.3%) also have the highest car-free household rates — entirely transit-dependent.

Crash Hotspot Analysis
Serious and fatal crashes (severity ≤2) within each station's 10-minute walkshed. Reduced car dependency from subway use could yield significant traffic safety co-benefits.
Data: PennDOT Crash Reportable Data 2024 · Philadelphia County
Bustleton records 18 crashes — nearly 3× the corridor mean of 6.2. Cottman (12) and Rising Sun (9) also well above average.

Indicator 03 / 03
Map · OSM Healthcare
Healthcare Access Gap
Number of healthcare facilities (hospitals, clinics, pharmacies) within each walkshed, combined with SVI to identify where vulnerable populations have the fewest medical resources.
Data: CDC SVI 2022 · OSM Healthcare Facilities
All 12 stations score Critical — the entire corridor lacks walkable healthcare. Most walksheds contain zero facilities. Subway gives residents fast access to medical resources outside the corridor.
